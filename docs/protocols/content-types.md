# Content Types

This document details the primary forms of media and content targeted by the Historical Postcard Archive.

## Supported Content Types

The system currently targets one primary form of media: **Vintage Historical Postcards**.
Specifically, the system filters for cards that contain **handwritten messages** from three distinct historical periods:

1. **WWI (World War I)**: 1914–1918
   - Postcards exchanged by soldiers at the front and families at home.
   - Field service postcards, military mail, and civilian correspondence during the Great War.

2. **WWII (World War II)**: 1939–1945
   - Military postcards, field post (Feldpost), V-mail equivalents.
   - Correspondence reflecting the global scale of the conflict.

3. **The Holocaust**:
   - Concentration camp mail (e.g., Dachau, Auschwitz, Buchenwald).
   - Ghetto correspondence (e.g., Warsaw, Lodz).
   - Postcards from Jewish persecution victims or Holocaust survivors.

## Content Criteria

- **Handwritten:** The system specifically targets cards with handwritten text (either cursive or block letters) rather than purely printed or blank unused postcards. The goal is to capture personal, historical narratives.
- **Originals:** Reprints or modern copies are discouraged; the focus is on original historical artifacts.
