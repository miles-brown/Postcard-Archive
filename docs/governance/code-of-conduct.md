# Community Code of Conduct

*Status: Active*

This document establishes moderation rules and acceptable behavior for users interacting with the platform. Note: Community features are currently slated for Phase 2 development.

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our archive a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Historical Integrity

When suggesting modifications to crowdsourced transcriptions (Phase 2):

1. Transcribe the text *exactly* as it was written by the historical author.
2. Do not censor profanity, ethnic slurs, or culturally insensitive language commonly found in primary-source historical military correspondence.
3. If an abbreviation is used, type the abbreviation exactly (e.g., do not natively expand "Lt." to "Lieutenant" without surrounding it in brackets if community rules are built to support expansions).

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the platform administrators. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, transcription suggestions, and issues that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will ban temporarily or permanently any contributors for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
